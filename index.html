SpiceRack — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

SpiceRack (“the app”, “we”) is a spice-pantry organizer. We built it to be private by default. This policy explains what data the app handles and what (little) leaves your device.

The short version

  • No account, no login. You don’t give us your name, email, or password.
  • Your spice data stays on your device. Spices, categories, racks, shopping list, and photos are stored locally on your phone. We don’t have a server that holds them.
  • We don’t sell your data and we don’t show ads.

What’s stored on your device

The following is saved locally (via the app’s on-device storage) and never sent to us:

  • Your spices and their details (name, brand, category, quantity, stock level, location, expiry date, notes, favorite status).
  • Categories and racks you create.
  • Your shopping list.
  • Photos you add to a spice (stored as a local file reference on your device).

You can remove this data at any time by deleting items in the app or uninstalling the app.

Information that leaves your device

SpiceRack only sends data off your device in these specific cases:

  1. Barcode lookups (Open Food Facts). When you scan a barcode, the barcode number is sent to the Open Food Facts API (openfoodfacts.org) to fetch product details. No personal information is attached. See their policy at world.openfoodfacts.org/privacy.
  2. Purchases (RevenueCat + Apple). If you start a Premium subscription, purchase processing is handled by Apple and by RevenueCat, our subscription provider. RevenueCat receives an anonymous identifier and purchase/receipt information to manage your subscription. It does not receive your spice data. See revenuecat.com/privacy.
  3. Apple Reminders export (optional). If you tap “Send to Apple Reminders”, the app creates reminders in your own Apple Reminders. This stays within Apple’s ecosystem on your device/iCloud; we don’t receive it.

Device permissions we request

  • Camera — to scan barcodes and (optionally) take a photo of a spice.
  • Photo Library — to let you choose an existing photo for a spice.
  • Notifications — to remind you (locally, on your device) before a spice expires. These reminders are scheduled on-device; we don’t send push notifications from a server.
  • Reminders — only if you use the optional “Send to Apple Reminders” feature.

Each permission is requested only when you use the related feature, and the app works without granting them.

Children’s privacy

SpiceRack is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Data retention & deletion

Because your data lives on your device, you control it. Deleting the app removes the app’s local data. Subscription records held by Apple/RevenueCat are governed by their policies.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we’ll update the “Last updated” date above.
